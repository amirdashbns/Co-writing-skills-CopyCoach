# Instagram Content Buckets

The taxonomy that organizes all Instagram content. Every carousel, caption, and post belongs to one of **4 master buckets**, each with sub-buckets. This is the spine of the whole system — pick the bucket first, then everything else (title, formula, caption, CTA) follows.

---

## The 4 Master Buckets

| Bucket | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **EDUCATE** | Help people understand the value of your work |
| **RELATE** | Connect with your people and humanize your business |
| **INSPIRE** | Show what's possible when they step in / say "yes" |
| **SELL** | Share your offers with the people who want/need them |

A healthy feed mixes all four. Educate and Relate build trust and reach; Inspire and Sell convert.

---

## EDUCATE (sub-buckets)

**Problem Awareness**
Make your people aware of a problem they're facing, why they're facing it, what's keeping them stuck, why what they've tried hasn't worked before, and what people get wrong. (The problem usually isn't what they think it is.)

**Actionable Tips**
Give simple steps to help give people clarity on what they need to do to move through a problem. How-tos, tips, step-by-step breakdowns, or tutorials.

**Value Shares**
Show your authority by sharing your knowledge. Framed as "truths" or "reveals," though they may be baseline knowledge within your industry.

**Plug-and-Play**
Do the work for your people. These "must-save" posts feel value-packed because they give away your work for free, even when it's just one tiny piece of a giant puzzle.

**Expert Showcase**
Lift the curtain on your expertise by showing why what you do is so good. Uses your own work to explain the "excellence" behind it. (Not about how you run your business — about your expertise around what you offer.)

**Business Insider**
Educate your audience on the backend of your business. What resources do you use and love? Why do you do things the way you do them? Great for people who want to be seen as leaders in their profession.

---

## RELATE (sub-buckets)

**Personal Introductions**
Introduce the likable human (or team) behind the business to make it feel personal. People buy from people.

**Story Shares**
Share the (often relatable) moments along your journey of business ownership — struggle, personal transformation, before & afters.

**Behind The Scenes**
Lift the curtain on how the magic happens so people can cheer on the human(s) behind the biz. Daily processes, day-to-day life.

**Engagement Invitations**
Create a concrete way for people to engage — questions, games, challenges, activities.

**Reflections & Recaps**
What's going on right now in your business? What's working, what's not, and why? What are you currently learning/processing/building?

**Just For Fun**
Show your personality, humor, or personal life through the lens of your business. (Emphasis on *through the lens of your business*.)

---

## INSPIRE (sub-buckets)

**Case Studies**
An in-depth study of one specific client/customer. Focus on one specific change: what happened when we/client stopped doing X and started doing Y.

**Struggle Share**
Share client "before" stories that make transformations visible and relatable. What were they struggling with BEFORE? What hesitations did they have? When did they almost quit?

**Turning Point**
What realizations/big shifts did people need to step into to start seeing results? Speak to false beliefs, what people get wrong, the solutions you offer.

**Before & Afters**
Show the before and after — visible, quantitative, qualitative, or told through a story.

**Visualizations & Projections**
Hypothetical case studies that help people see the value in jumping into buying from you.

**Portfolio Shares**
Share the detailed deliverables of a completed offer or product.

---

## SELL (sub-buckets)

**Offer Pitch**
Connect the problem people are having to an offer. Works best when "live launching" with limited enrollment, or with time-sensitive launch perks.

**The Only Option**
Give insight into why your offer includes what it includes — and what it doesn't. What do others get wrong? What do you do better?

**Self-Selection**
Center around the question "Is this you?" Lead with SPECIFIC pain points and problems so people self-select into your offer.

**Sliding Door Stories**
Stories that help people understand the drawbacks of NOT jumping on this offer — what alternative they'll be missing out on, why this is the best option.

**Excitement Builders**
Giveaways, sweepstakes, challenges, special bonuses — increase excitement around an offer and invite more people into the promotion.

**Lead Generators**
Point to freebies, resources, or discounts. Used as email list builders or DM incentives.

**FAObjections (FAOs)**
Directly answer frequently asked *objections* (not FAQs about what's included). "Is this right for me? What if I don't feel ready? What if it feels like a big investment?"

## Worked Examples (how a bucket becomes a topic)

These show how each sub-bucket translates into a concrete post idea for a real business.

### Example: Wedding Cake Baker — audience: brides on a budget, niche: floral designs
- **Problem Awareness:** How to feed a large wedding on a minimalist budget
- **Actionable Tips:** A specific flower decoration technique to DIY a floral wedding cake
- **Value Shares:** Why your fondant icing is bulging
- **Plug-and-Play:** The 5 baking tools you need to decorate a cake like a professional
- **Expert Showcase:** How you design a cake for couples with totally different flavor preferences
- **Case Study:** What happened when a client went with cupcakes instead of a sheet cake
- **Self-Selection:** If you want a maximalist floral wedding cake for under $300…
- **FAO:** Why floral wedding cakes are more expensive

### Example: Brand Photographer — audience: product-based businesses, niche: sustainable brands
- **Problem Awareness:** The problem with stock photos
- **Actionable Tips:** How to choose props for your brand photoshoot
- **Value Shares:** The secret to making your sustainable brand look cool
- **Plug-and-Play:** The exact camera settings for a backlit product photo

> Use the same logic for any business: take the audience's specific problem + experience level, then generate one focused idea per sub-bucket. See `brainstorm-questions.md` for prompts that surface these ideas.
