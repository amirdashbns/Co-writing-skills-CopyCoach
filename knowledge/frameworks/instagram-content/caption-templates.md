# Caption Templates

The caption that accompanies a carousel. **The caption is NOT a repeat of the slides** — if it obviously restates the carousel, people stop reading captions. Instead: add to the information, tell a story that emphasizes the point, invite engagement, or at minimum say it a different way.

## Caption-writing rules
- **Don't repeat the carousel.** Expand on it, tell a behind-it story, or add a fresh angle.
- **Open with a hook** (its own job — first line decides if they tap "more").
- **End with a CTA** (see `plug-and-play-ctas.md`). Prioritize comments, saves, shares.
- **Caption SEO:** use keywords related to your business 3-4 times.
- **Voice override:** these templates capture *structure and persuasion beats*. Fill every line in the user's voice (`/context/voice-dna.json`) — never the template's voice.

## Universal caption skeleton
Most high-selling captions follow this arc (adapt the beats to the bucket):
1. **Hook** — a relatable opener, bold claim, question, or curiosity line that earns the "…more" tap
2. **Relate / validate** — show you get their experience ("If you're anything like me…")
3. **The real problem or truth** — name what's actually going on (often not what they think)
4. **Build the case** — why it's true; examples, contrast (what works vs. what doesn't), a stat or story
5. **The shift / what to do** — the reframe or the small next step
6. **CTA** — one clear action
7. **#hashtags** — 3-5, mix of popular + niche

## Worked structural example (Problem Awareness, C5 — "why it makes sense they're still struggling")

> **Hook:** "I see it ALL. THE. TIME." (a confident, pattern-naming opener)
> **Relate:** name the action they took that didn't work, and paint the time/money/energy they put in
> **Tension:** "But [timeframe] later… [how it falls short]"
> **The truth:** the action CAN work, *but* [the limitation / why it didn't work for them]
> **Reassure:** that one bad result doesn't mean results aren't possible (optional stat/proof)
> **The shift:** the one thing they should do instead + why it works
> **CTA** + **#hashtags**

Use this as a model for how the beats flow, then rewrite every line in the user's voice. The same skeleton adapts to any bucket by swapping the "real problem / truth / shift" beats for that bucket's job (e.g. a Case Study leads with a client + result; an Offer Pitch leads with the problem→offer connection).
