# 16 Killer Carousel Formulas

Slide-by-slide structures for Instagram carousels (up to 10 slides), organized by content bucket. Each formula gives the *purpose* of each slide plus prompt/sentence-starter options.

**How to use:** Pick the formula that matches your bucket + goal. Slide 1 is always a clickable post title (see `post-title-formulas.md`). Slide 10 is always a CTA (see `plug-and-play-ctas.md`). Fill every slide in the user's voice (see `/context/voice-dna.json`) — never the template's voice.

**The 3 pillars (apply to every formula):**
- **Value** — actively useful for *this* audience (their specific problem, experience level, knowledge of your offers)
- **Momentum** — every slide either leads into the next or gives enough value to keep swiping. First 3 slides matter most. Use seeds of curiosity ("here's what we mean," "let's look at an example"), direct words ("swipe," "next"), or arrows.
- **Readability** — headings (tips/takeaways, not titles) + subheadings (one-liners) + body text (≤3 lines). Third-grade reading level. Don't say in five words what you can say in one.

**The hourglass flow:** Slide 1 = most zoomed out (big picture/curiosity) → zoom in (context/new perspective) → get specific (the meat: examples, advice, details) → zoom back out (big takeaway) → connect to you (CTA).

## EDUCATE FORMULA 1 — *Perfect for: Actionable Tips*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.** e.g. "[Struggling] [to/with] [achieve goal]?" / "X [actionable tips] you [should/need to] know" / "How to [action verb] [end goal] →"
- **Slide 2 — Give beginner context / distill topic (optional).** "You may have heard…" / "You already know…" / "New [people] always ask me…" / "Let's talk about…" / "It's probably because…" / "ICYMI —"
- **Slides 3-8 — Actionable tips.** "[Take this action.]" + description, examples, and/or additional tips to help them complete the action more effectively.
- **Slide 9 — Big takeaway (optional).** "Remember: …" / "Stop {X} and start {Y}." / "If you want {result}, you need to {action}." / "It's not about {this}. It's about {that}." / "The goal of {X} isn't to {result}. It's to {result}."
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## EDUCATE FORMULA 2 — *Perfect for: Expert Showcase*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.** "How to use [expertise/tool/idea] [to/for] [reach goal]" / "Steal my/our [process] for [task/result]" / "Why you should always [complete task]"
- **Slide 2 — Ask a question.** "What's the deal with…?" / "Do you ever wonder…?" / "Have you ever noticed…?" / "Does {topic} really matter?"
- **Slide 3 — Add context to your question / offer an example / offer a reframe.** "Think of it like this…" / "Say you're…" / "Here's what I mean…" / "You {do/don't do X} and think {objection}. But…"
- **Slide 4 — Distill the 'aha moment.'** "Because…" / "The truth is…" / "The thing is…"
- **Slides 5-8 — Examples (with commentary explaining your choices!).** "I {take this action} because…" / "Instead of… {take this action}." / "See how {this happens} when you {take action}?" / "{This} is better than {that} because…"
- **Slide 9 — Big takeaway (optional).** (Same options as Formula 1.)
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## EDUCATE FORMULA 3 — *Perfect for: Value Share / Actionable Tips*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.** "The truth about [topic]." / "[Topic], finally explained." / "What no one tells you about [topic]"
- **Slide 2 — A fact or statistic.** "Did you know…" / "Fact: …" / "According to…" (Choose the message first, then find the stat. Pick the most surprising framing — "80%" not "4 out of 5.")
- **Slide 3 — A truth, revealed.** "{Achieving goal} isn't about {this}. It's about {that}." / "So clearly, {takeaway from the stat}." / "Which is why {what the fact explains}."
- **Slide 4 — Big promise / topic distilled.** "Here's how to…" / "So, what SHOULD you do? {Take this action.}" / "{Reaching your goal} won't {look like this}. It will {look like this}."
- **Slides 5-8 — Examples / actionable tips.** "For example…" / "Instead of…try…" / "#X: {Take this action}" / "Next time {you approach topic}, ask yourself…" (Frame as before/after where possible.)
- **Slide 9 — Big takeaway (optional).**
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## EDUCATE FORMULA 4 — *Perfect for: Plug-and-Play*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.**
- **Slide 2 — Breakdown of plug-and-play categories (optional).** "If you're a… {list identities}" / "If your {noun} is… {list identities}" / "For… {list categories}" / "For every type of {category}"
- **Slides 3-9 — Plug-and-play content.** "{Category title, if applicable}" OR "{Number}" + the plug-and-play content. (Group content by an *identity-based* category to make it original — e.g. "5 wedding color schemes for soft summers" rather than the overused "fall wedding color schemes.")
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## EDUCATE FORMULA 5 — *Perfect for: Problem Awareness*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.** "[Common advice] is [overrated/a waste of time/etc.]." / "Should you [take this action]?" / "Why [action/product] [could be] [having negative effect] →"
- **Slide 2 — The real problem.** "If you find yourself {taking these actions}, you could be {experiencing this unintentional consequence}." / "You're {doing these things 'right'}. So why are you still {struggling}?" / "Are you {how you're unintentionally contributing to the problem}?" (The problem usually isn't what they think — focus on what *they* are doing/saying that contributes.)
- **Slide 3 — Spill the truth / offer a perspective shift.** "The truth is…" / "Think of it this way:" / "It seems like {what they think the problem is}. But {what the real problem is}." / "What if {new truth}?"
- **Slide 4 — Back up your argument.** "{Reaching goal} doesn't look like {X}. It looks like {Y}." / "Every time you {take action}, you might be {seeing result}. But you're also {negative impact}." (Show what works AND what doesn't.)
- **Slide 5 — Make a connection / empathize / give context on why their belief or action makes sense.** "I get it. You want… But…" / "And it makes sense. You've been taught {false promise}." (Can move earlier — e.g. after slide 2 — for touchy topics, so they don't feel judged.)
- **Slide 6 — The big lesson.** "But if you want {different result}, {what they need to do differently}." (Put the agency back in their hands — something *they* can do.)
- **Slide 7 — Possible solutions (optional).** "So instead of {old action}, try {alternative}." / "Here's how to {do things differently} →" (If you have no plug-and-play examples, this is a great place to tie in your offer as the solution.)
- **Slide 8 — Examples (optional).** "Instead of…Try…" / "#X: {Take this action}"
- **Slide 9 — Big takeaway (optional).** Circle back to what they thought the problem was vs. their new thought process.
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## RELATE FORMULA 2 — *Perfect for: Story Shares*

## RELATE FORMULA 3 — *Perfect for: Behind the Scenes*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.** "A day in the life of a [job title]" / "How I/we [complete task] as a [job title]" / "I [took action] [timeline]. Here's what happened →"

- **Slide 9 — The final product/solution/outcome.** "And voilà! That's how…" / "Here's the final product:" / "It turned out {summarize the results}."
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## SELL FORMULA 1 — *Perfect for: The Only Option*

- **Slide 1 — Clickable post title.** "The problem with [alternate solution]." / "Why [other action] failed [optional: you]." / "Why [other action] [doesn't work]."
- **Slide 2 — Context.** "Here's a mistake I see so many {clients} make:" / "People often ask me {isn't (alternative)} {a better solution}?"
- **Slide 3 — Why it seems like a good alternative.** "I know {alternative} seems like {a possibility}." / "I get it — {alternative} seems like {a great solution} because…" / "There's no question {alternative} is a great option if you want… {list}"
- **Slide 4 — The big-picture explanation of why it's not a good alternative.** "And it can be…IF {you meet qualifications}." / "But if you want {the big desire}, then…" / "The problem is: {describe the problem with alternatives}."
- **Slide 5 — Agitate why it's not a good alternative.** "Which is why {specific ways people don't find success with alternatives}." / "And {ways alternatives can make the problem worse}."
- **Slide 6 — What the alternative is missing.** "Because if you want {real results}, you need {what your offer gets right that others get wrong}." / "Because {alternative} is {missing this fundamental element}."
- **Slide 7 — The only option.** "{Your offer} is the only {service/product} that will {give you these specific benefits}." / "I made {offer} because I saw so many people struggling with {problem}…"
- **Slide 8 — Path they could keep pursuing + why.** "You could keep…" / "If you {valid reasons to seek alternatives}…"
- **Slide 9 — Path they should start pursuing if they want different results.** "Or you could…" / "But if you want {results your offer does better}…"
- **Slide 10 — CTA.**

## Related-content tip

For any post, generate the *next step* and *previous step* a person might take, and make a post for each. (E.g. a post on "how to set up a barbell for hip thrusts" → step back: "dumbbell glute exercises if you don't have a barbell"; step forward: "how to do hip thrusts with proper form.") This builds a feed that keeps an audience progressing through your content.
