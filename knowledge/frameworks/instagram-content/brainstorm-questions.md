# Content Brainstorm Questions

Idea-generation prompts organized by bucket and sub-bucket. Use these in the **idea step** (before writing) to surface a specific, valuable topic. A carousel can only be as clear and valuable as the idea behind it.

**How to use:** Answer questions for the chosen sub-bucket against `/context/icp.json` (the audience's problems, experience level, language) and `/context/business-profile.json` (offers). Pick the one specific problem the post will solve, then move to the title + formula.

---

## EDUCATE

**Problem Awareness**
- What's the biggest challenge for beginners just starting out in your niche?
- What feels hard for your audience on a daily basis? What's taking up a ton of their time?
- How does your audience feel stuck? What do they *not yet realize* is keeping them stuck?
- What do you immediately flag as a problem that they haven't realized is a problem yet?
- What's a big mistake you see others in your profession making/teaching?
- What have they tried before that hasn't worked, and why didn't it work?
- What's the most common mistake people make before they hire/buy from you? What are the consequences?
- What questions do you get in your DMs?

**Actionable Tips**
- Take a specific problem: what are 3-8 steps to solve it? Teach ONE sub-step deeply.
- What is your audience DIY-ing? How can you help them DIY faster or more successfully?
- What hack or workaround did you discover that makes it easier?
- Where is your audience wasting time/energy? What should they do instead?
- What do they think will be difficult that's actually simple?
- What would you teach if they paid you $10,000 for 5 minutes? What are the BASIC ESSENTIALS?

**Value Shares**
- What do you commonly see people doing incorrectly? What's a better way?
- What task do people make more complicated than it needs to be?
- What are complex concepts in your industry you can break down into simple terms?
- What "uncomfortable" truth can you call out?
- What bad advice does your industry give? What would YOU say instead?
- What advice is only PARTIALLY true, or only true sometimes?

**Plug-and-Play**
- What daily task can you knock off your audience's to-do list or make easier?
- What's on your "List of Essentials" for your audience?
- What can you give them they'd reference every day/week/month/year?
- What different archetypes/identities does your audience have? Make something for each.

**Expert Showcase**
- If you were teaching a substitute to do your task, what guide would you give them?
- Take a project: how did you do it? Why did you make the choices you made?
- What common task do you do better than most? Show the common way vs. your way.
- How do you complete a complex/intimidating task? Simplify it.

**Business Insider**
- What goals have you reached in your business? What were the main things you did?
- What does the backend of your business / client process look like? Why?
- What automations or processes keep your business running smoothly?
- What business advice do you outright ignore? What do you do differently?

## RELATE

**Personal Introductions**
- What's your story? How did you start doing this work? What was the "aha" moment?
- What are 5-10 fun things everyone should know about you?
- Describe the type of people you'd REALLY love to work with.
- Share your company values — how do you practice them in real time?

**Story Shares**
- What's a moment you thought "I don't think I can do this"? How did you get through it?
- A time you worked really hard and it didn't pay off — tell the whole story. What did you learn?
- A big win moment — what was it, how did you celebrate, what got you there?
- The lesson in business that took you longest to learn — share the story.

**Behind The Scenes**
- What does your day-to-day look like? What tasks, and how do you complete them?
- What tools/programs keep your business running?
- What have you been working on behind the scenes that you haven't released yet?

**Engagement Invitations**
- Ask your audience to self-identify from options (creative or direct).
- Mock up 3 versions of something; invite a vote.
- Ask directly: what do they want to see next? What do they need help with?
- "Can you [do this better]?" using an industry prompt.

**Reflections & Recaps**
- What's something new you're trying that IS working? Share results.
- What's something that's NOT working? Why, and how would you iterate?
- Recap your last launch/month/quarter — revenue, best-seller, lessons.

**Just For Fun**
- What are your favorite things to do apart from your business? How do hobbies influence your work?
- What are you/your team obsessed with lately — through the lens of your business?
- A sarcastic how-to full of "bad" advice (clearly for fun).

## INSPIRE

**Case Studies**
- What did you STOP doing that changed results? Make it quantifiable.
- What transformations do clients mention? Be specific — quantify (revenue, sales, conversion, followers, engagement, DMs, time spent, pain level 1-10, etc.).

**Struggle Share**
- What did your client tell you they were struggling with before? Their exact words (find DMs).
- What emotional/financial hesitations did they have? What had they tried before?
- What would they have missed out on had they skipped your solution?

**Turning Point**
- What misconceptions do clients have before working with you?
- In what ways do other solutions fall short? Why does it make sense they haven't worked?
- What mindset shift do people need to start seeing results? What must they let go of?

**Before & After**
- Compare a first draft/sketch with the final product.
- What changed visually/emotionally/financially/mentally for your client?
- Tell the "before" story and the "after" story. (Ways to show: photos, annotated photos, charts, quotes, timelines.)

**Visualizations & Projections**
- How would your client's life look in 1 month / 6 months / 1 year if they bought now?
- What would their life look like if they DON'T invest now? What problems persist?
- How would the investment pay off? How long to make it back? (Show 3 realistic scenarios.)
- Who is this realistic for? What do they need to already have figured out?

**Portfolio Shares**
- A project you LOVED that shows off your expertise — what about it do you love?
- A detail you're especially proud of / that took a long time to perfect.
- An overview of everything you deliver (tangible + intangible).

## SELL

**Offer Pitch**
- How is your offer the PERFECT solution for one specific problem/desire?
- When they buy, what immediately changes? In 6 months? In 1 year?
- What's included, and what problem does each inclusion solve?
- What proof do you have it works?
- Why should they buy NOW, not in 6 months? (bonuses, limited spots, enrollment window)

**The Only Option**
- What are competitors offering that you decidedly are NOT — and why?
- What does your offer include that others don't? What does it do better?
- What makes your offer the ONLY choice?
- What could they keep doing instead, and why is that the wrong choice?

**Self-Selection**
- Who is your offer PERFECT for? Who should run to checkout?
- What 4-6 specific day-to-day problems does your offer address?
- How does someone know it's right for them (experience level, struggle, hope)?
- Show each problem in 10 words or less, in their own words.

**Sliding Door Stories**
- What did clients try before you that didn't work — and how were they worse off after?
- A time YOU didn't invest in something and regretted it. What could you have avoided?

**Excitement Builders & Lead Generators**
- A giveaway: like + follow + tag a friend to enter.
- A low-cost bonus offered free for a limited time.
- A 5/10/30-day challenge that builds a new habit (option: discount during it).
- A small "secret" insight you could give your audience as a freebie.

**FAObjections (FAOs)**
- What objections do clients most often come with?
- Money excuses — how do you make it affordable/risk-free, or explain the value?
- Time excuses — how do you show feasibility (e.g. recordings)?
- Ease excuses — how is it easier than they expect?
- In what ways do people tell themselves they're "not ready"? What limiting beliefs do they hold?
